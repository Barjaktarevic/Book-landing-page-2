What do we know about the film release so far?

Click below to find out!

On December 15, 2021, The Hollywood Reporter broke the news that Denis Villeneuve is set to direct a movie adaption of 2001: A Space Odyssey author Arthur C. Clarke's classic novel Rendezvous With Rama.

Villeneuve's film will be produced by Alcon Entertainment, which also produced his previous films Prisoners and Blade Runner: 2049. Freeman and McCreary will also produce through Revelations.

No indication has been given that Villeneuve has any intention to adapt anything other than the original book, especially since for over two decades, the rights to the original book only have been owned by Revelations Entertainment, Morgan Freeman and Lori McCreary's production company.

As of now, there’s no news about any potential writers or actors connected to the project, so we’ll just have to wait and see.

No information has been released yet, as the funding most certainly hasn't yet been agreed upon, but we can expect the budget to be similar to Dune's at around $150 million.

In terms of a release date, Villeneuve is already set to direct Dune: Part 2 as well as executive produce and direct a few episodes of Dune: The Sisterhood, a prequel series on HBO Max, so it seems Rendezvous With Rama won't be hitting theaters anytime soon. But all good things come to those who wait.
